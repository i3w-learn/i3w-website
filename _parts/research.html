How we research

The principles underneath the notes.

Four habits that keep our points of view honest as the product moves.

01

Start from the student, not the slide

Every note begins with a real moment — a doubt at night, a parent's hesitation, a student giving up. Observation first, abstraction after.

02

Measure what counts as a win

We define the value action before we build. If a feature can't move a real learning metric, it doesn't earn the screen.

03

Safety is a design input, not a patch

Building for minors, guardrails sit up front — tone, redirection, escalation — not bolted on after something breaks.

04

Hold every position loosely

Field notes, not commandments. When a student says otherwise, the note changes — we'd rather be corrected fast than right slowly.
